Hi! I am an ML engineer at AWS, Annarapuna Labs working on distributed training of large scale LLMs on Trainium (AWS Machine Learning acceleration) architecture. My current work involves setting accuracy standards for the Distributed Training team, validating training dynamics and conducting pre-training/SFT evaluations against GPU benchmarks. Previously, I graduated with a Masters(thesis) in Computer Science, from Stony Brook University, where I was fortunate to by advised by Dr. H. Andrew Schwartz.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Reinforcement Learning and Computational Social Science. My long-term vision is to develop AI models that are safer, socially responsible, and equipped with social intelligence, enabling them to understand and navigate complex social dynamics effectively.

Outside of work and research, I love to read, music and play my guitar.

All Hands on Deck


Processed 110 GB of raw semi-structured US crime data (50M rows), and performed hypothesis testing to find statistically significant socio-economic features which have a high correlation with corruption offenses. Performed an exploratory analysis of corruption offenses in US across country and state level granularities and clustered similar states into 4 different groups using K-means.
